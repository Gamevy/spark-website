Collaboration Superpowers: Stories Of Remote Teams Doing Great Things

Book now!
Limited spaces

We all know that team collaboration is essential, yet also that the ability to choose where you work (and when) is an important part of personal autonomy. Moreover, in today's globally connected business, increasingly, many teams are "distributed by default." So how do we make remote teams work? How do we build relationships and trust and raise the quality of our communication?

Lisette Sutherland of Happy Melly in the Netherlands and Elinor Slomba of Arts Interstices in Connecticut have been collecting stories of remote teams with collaboration superpowers. Here they present the very best ideas - the successes and failures of high-performing remote teams.

Learn from peers who are bridging distance, building trust and getting great things done together.

About Lisette and Elinor

Lisette creates online collaborative communities, specializing in web-based collaboration tools and online community management. Her goal is to get the best people working together regardless of location. She currently works as Happy Melly's Community Builder, coordinating the global marketing efforts and connect pockets of knowledge all over the world to to turn work into a great and gratifying experience.


Spark The Change is organised by Software Acumen, based in Cambridge and Gamevy, based in London. Spark The Change is an official Dare Experience, which is a Happy Melly Brand.
