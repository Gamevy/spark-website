Intellectual Property. What’s Wrong with the Concept?

Book now!
limited tickets

Intellectual property law is intended to protect innovation and creativity, yet in recent years the over-enthusiastic use of patents has acted to stymy it.

In contrast, in recent years we have seen an explosion of open source, creative commons and free content and these alternatives have often proved more successful. Many makers now seek alternative methods to make money from their creativity without necessarily protecting the material itself. David Chan explores how firms have traditionally tried to protect and exploit ideas and discusses alternative models that are likely to become more common.

Thought-provoking and intended to stimulate some lively debate, David’s talk will challenge ideas for companies and makers alike. For anyone interested in innovation and competition, this talk will prove enlightening.

About David Chan

David Chan is the Director of the Information Leadership Network, Cass Business School. David was one of the first board-level IT Directors in the UK and the winner of the Royal Television Society's Award for Innovation in 1999.

In a leadership career spanning 3 decades, David has worked for both vendor and buyer across many industry sectors. His focus is now on improving the advanced education provision for Information Leaders and raising the profile of Information Leadership to the business community as a whole.
